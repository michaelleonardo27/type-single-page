History of the font

LAB DJR's foray into OpenType Font Variations marks a pioneering exploration into a novel font format, housing a diverse array of font styles within a single file. Specializing in pixel font design, LAB showcases a unique approach where each glyph is meticulously crafted from a repeating block, allowing for dynamic changes in shape. This innovative design, achieved through the manipulation of pixel size, roundness, bevel, and quantity, presents an expansive range of possibilities and provides a glimpse into the potential of variable fonts. As a letter artist, DJR possesses a talent for creating letters in various shapes and sizes, catering to both custom and retail typeface designs. Originally from Los Angeles, DJR embarked on the journey of drawing typefaces during their time at Hampshire College. In 2007, DJR further honed their skills by joining The Font Bureau, mastering the intricacies of bézier-wrangling. Presently, DJR operates their own foundry, where they publish visually imaginative and technically advanced designs, including notable typefaces such as Fit, Manicotti, Input, LAB DJR, and Bungee. Residing in Western Massachusetts with their partner Emily and their two dogs, Sophie and Lily, DJR continues to contribute to the world of typography, showcasing a passion for creativity and technical excellence in each endeavor.

Weight: Adjust the thickness of characters, from thin to bold, for establishing hierarchy and emphasis. Width: Modify the horizontal proportions of characters, from condensed to expanded forms, optimizing text layout. Optical Size: Adapt proportions and details of characters based on their size within the text, ensuring legibility across different point sizes. Italic Angle: Control the slant of characters, from slight italicization to pronounced oblique styles, for stylistic diversity. Contrast: Alter the variation between thick and thin strokes within characters, from low to high contrast, influencing the overall texture and visual impact. Grade: Adjust overall size and weight proportion of characters, from fine to heavy grades, for designing display typefaces with different visual impact. X-height: Modify the relative height of lowercase characters, from short to tall forms, influencing legibility and overall typographic texture. Terminal Style: Change the shape and endings of characters, from flat to round terminals, adding subtle visual interest and character. With these customizable attributes, LAB DJR's variable font empowers designers to precisely tailor typographic elements to their needs, ensuring optimal aesthetics, readability, and expressive versatility across various design applications.
